## Copyright notice

Send a written notice containing:

1. your physical or electronic signature, or that of a person authorized to act for the rights owner;
2. identification of the copyrighted work, or a representative list if multiple works are involved;
3. identification and location of the material or LOOK5 link at issue, with enough information to find it;
4. your name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address;
5. a statement that you have a good-faith belief that the disputed use is not authorized by the owner, its agent, or the law; and
6. a statement that the notice is accurate and, under penalty of perjury, that you are the owner or authorized to act for the owner.

LOOK5 may request missing information. When appropriate, LOOK5 may remove user content or disable a source link while evaluating the notice. If the material is hosted by an external website, LOOK5 cannot remove it from that website.

## Counter-notice

An affected user may send a written counter-notice containing:

1. a physical or electronic signature;
2. identification of the material removed or disabled and its former location;
3. a statement under penalty of perjury that the user has a good-faith belief the material was removed or disabled because of mistake or misidentification;
4. the user’s name, address, and telephone number; and
5. the jurisdiction and service-of-process consent required by applicable law.

Non-routine ownership, fair-use, counter-notice, court-order, trademark, or impersonation disputes may require legal review.

## Repeat infringement

LOOK5 may terminate accounts of repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances and records substantiated notices and related enforcement.

## DMCA registration status

LOOK5 has not registered or claimed a U.S. Copyright Office Designated DMCA Agent for v0.1.0 and does not represent that it qualifies for a Section 512 safe harbor. This contact and process remain available for copyright concerns.
