Effective: July 15, 2026

LOOK5 is operated by Nicolas Conry. This Policy explains how LOOK5 handles information in its mobile app, service, support channels, and related public pages.

## Information LOOK5 collects

When you create an account, LOOK5 collects your email address, lowercase username, authentication and session information, optional profile bio, privacy-default setting, and account timestamps. Authentication is provided through Supabase. LOOK5 application records do not store a readable copy of your password.

For each runway look, an account may save one interaction containing some or all of:

- a 0.5–5.0 rating;
- review text of up to 240 characters;
- up to ten one-word tags;
- favorite status;
- a public/private setting;
- account and look identifiers; and
- creation and update timestamps.

If you contact LOOK5, information you choose to send may include your email, username, message, screenshots, rights documentation, and correspondence history.

## Public and private interactions

One visibility setting applies to the rating, review, tags, and favorite in a saved interaction.

If an interaction is public, eligible information may appear with the username on look pages, public activity, rankings, or other public surfaces supported by the current app.

If an interaction is private:

- it remains visible to its owner;
- the username and interaction are not displayed as public activity;
- private review text is not displayed or counted as a public review; and
- active ratings, tags, and favorites may still contribute to community averages, counts, rankings, and popular tags without public attribution.

“Anonymous aggregate” or “unattributed” describes the public result. The underlying private record remains linked to the account so its owner can view, change, or delete it. Remove a field if you do not want that signal included in active aggregates.

## Reports and moderation evidence

An authenticated user may report another user’s eligible public review. A report may retain the reporter and reported-account identifiers, look identifier, a snapshot of the reported username, review text, rating and review-update time, status, and action timestamps.

An authenticated user may also report public profile content. That report may retain the reported username, bio, public tags, relevant identifiers, status, actions, and audit timestamps.

These snapshots are non-public moderation evidence. They may remain after the live content is edited, removed, made private, hidden, or affected by account deletion. Reporting does not automatically remove content.

## Blocking

Blocking is directional. It hides the blocked account’s eligible public reviews, Latest Activity, and public-profile activity from the blocker. It does not notify the blocked account, delete underlying content, or change what uninvolved people can see. Global unattributed aggregates may remain unchanged.

## Controlled seed/test data

LOOK5 may contain internally controlled seed/test accounts and interactions so features can be tested with meaningful content. Seed ratings, reviews, tags, favorites, and activity may appear on product surfaces or contribute to aggregates under the same visibility rules. Controlled data is internally marked and separable from real-user data. It is not evidence of independent user adoption.

## How information is used

LOOK5 uses information to:

- create, authenticate, and secure accounts;
- provide catalog browsing and account features;
- save visibility choices and interactions;
- display eligible public content and community aggregates;
- receive and investigate reports, rights requests, and abuse;
- maintain catalog provenance and external-source links;
- provide support;
- operate, test, debug, secure, and improve LOOK5; and
- comply with law and protect users, LOOK5, and others.

## Analytics, advertising, and TestFlight

The current app does not include an advertising SDK, cross-app tracking, or a dedicated third-party analytics SDK. When you open a verified external catalog source, LOOK5 records a limited first-party event containing the source or collection identifier, destination domain, app build, screen, timestamp, and—if you are signed in—your account identifier. It does not store the full destination URL, review text, tags, email address, advertising identifier, or device fingerprint in this event. Core product records also contain timestamps needed to operate the service.

During TestFlight, Apple may collect and provide LOOK5 with crash, usage, device, and feedback information under Apple’s TestFlight privacy terms.

LOOK5 does not sell personal information, share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, use it for targeted advertising, include an advertising SDK, or use it for cross-app tracking in v0.1.0.

## Service providers and external sites

LOOK5 uses Supabase for database, authentication, and related infrastructure, and Apple for app distribution and TestFlight. These providers process information to perform services for LOOK5.

External source buttons open websites or apps controlled by other organizations. LOOK5 stores catalog metadata and source URLs but does not currently host third-party runway imagery or video. External operators have their own privacy practices and terms.

## Retention and deletion

Active account and interaction information is kept while needed to provide the service.

In-app account deletion disables access, releases the username, clears the bio, makes interactions private and soft-deleted, removes public activity, and excludes those interactions from active community calculations. Former credentials cannot be used to sign in.

Some information may remain after public removal when reasonably necessary:

- soft-deleted rows during controlled deletion, security, and backup processes;
- moderation report snapshots and action evidence;
- support, copyright, privacy, fraud, and security records; and
- provider logs and backups until their operational retention periods expire.

Legal holds, safety matters, fraud or security investigations, and applicable law may require longer retention. LOOK5’s approved numeric retention targets will not be represented as operational until its provider logs, purge operations, and backup rolloff have been verified.

## Choices

You may:

- browse public areas without an account;
- select public or private visibility for each interaction;
- change or remove interaction fields;
- clear an interaction from active product data;
- manage blocked accounts in Profile → Settings → Blocked accounts;
- delete your account in Profile → Settings → Delete account; and
- email [look5founder@gmail.com](mailto:look5founder@gmail.com) for support or an applicable access, correction, deletion, or privacy request.

LOOK5 may need to verify identity before responding to an account-specific request.

## Age

LOOK5 does not offer adult content, is not directed to children, and requires users to be at least 18 to create an account. Public browsing remains available without an account.

## Security

LOOK5 uses access controls, row-level database security, authenticated server operations, and HTTPS service endpoints intended to protect information. No system is completely secure. Send suspected security issues to [look5founder@gmail.com](mailto:look5founder@gmail.com) without including passwords or access tokens.

## Changes and contact

LOOK5 may update this Policy as the product, providers, or law change. The effective date will be updated and additional notice or consent provided when required.

Privacy, support, security, and copyright contact: [look5founder@gmail.com](mailto:look5founder@gmail.com).
